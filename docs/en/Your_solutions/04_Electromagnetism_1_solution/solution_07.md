

## 7. Cyclotron Motion
**The Problem:** We shoot an electron out of a "gun" using 5000 Volts. The electron flies into a room that has a Magnetic Field ($B = 0.1$ Tesla). Magnetic fields make moving electric charges spin in perfect circles. We need to find the radius (size) of that circle.

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1.  **Find the Speed ($v$):**
    First, we need to know how fast the 5000 Volts made the electron go. The electrical energy converts into kinetic energy (movement energy).
    $$ \text{Electrical Energy} = \text{Kinetic Energy} $$
    $$ q \cdot V = \frac{1}{2} m \cdot v^2 $$
    To find speed ($v$), we rearrange the formula:
    $$ v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot q \cdot V}{m}} $$
    *   $q$ = $1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ (electron charge)
    *   $V$ = 5000
    *   $m$ = $9.11 \times 10^{-31}$ (electron mass)
    $$ v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot (1.6 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot 5000}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}} \approx 4.19 \times 10^7 \text{ meters per second} $$
    *(That's extremely fast!)*
2.  **Find the Radius ($r$):**
    When moving in a circle, the magnetic force acts as the "leash" (centripetal force) keeping it in the circle.
    $$ \text{Magnetic Force} = \text{Centripetal Force} $$
    $$ q \cdot v \cdot B = \frac{m \cdot v^2}{r} $$
    Rearrange to find $r$:
    $$ r = \frac{m \cdot v}{q \cdot B} $$
    $$ r = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31}) \cdot (4.19 \times 10^7)}{(1.6 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot 0.1} \approx 0.00238 \text{ meters} $$

**Final Answer:** The electron spins in a circle with a radius of 0.00238 meters (which is 2.38 millimeters).
