

## 5. Field Levitation
**The Problem:** Gravity is trying to pull a proton down to the floor. We want to create an upward "Electric Field" (an invisible upward electric wind) strong enough to hold the proton in the air, making it float (levitate). How strong must this field be?

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1.  **The Logic:** For the proton to float, the upward pull must exactly match the downward pull.
    $$ \text{Upward Electric Force} = \text{Downward Gravity Force} $$
2.  **The Formulas:**
    *   Electric Force is charge multiplied by field strength: $F_e = q \cdot E$
    *   Gravity Force is mass multiplied by gravity: $F_g = m \cdot g$
    So, $q \cdot E = m \cdot g$
3.  **Solve for Electric Field ($E$):**
    We want $E$ by itself, so we divide by $q$.
    $$ E = \frac{m \cdot g}{q} $$
4.  **Plug in the Numbers:**
    *   Mass of proton ($m$) = $1.67 \times 10^{-27}$ kg
    *   Earth's gravity ($g$) = 9.8 m/s²
    *   Charge of proton ($q$) = $1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ C
    $$ E = \frac{1.67 \times 10^{-27} \cdot 9.8}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}} \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ V/m} $$

**Final Answer:** We need an upward electric field of $1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ V/m}$.
