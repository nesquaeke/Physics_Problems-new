

# Section 4: Electromagnetism I - Detailed Solutions

**Before we start, here is a simple dictionary for physics terms we will use:**
*   **Charge ($q$ or $Q$):** Think of charges as magnets. Positive (+) and Negative (-) charges. Opposites attract, same charges repel (push away). Measured in Coulombs (C).
*   **Force ($F$):** A push or a pull. Measured in Newtons (N).
*   **Electric Field ($E$):** An invisible "aura" around a charge that pushes or pulls other charges.
*   **Constant ($k$):** A fixed number in nature we always use for electric math. It is exactly $9 \times 10^9$.

## 1. Coulomb's Law
**The Problem:** Imagine a square drawn on the floor. Each side is 1.0 meter long. You put four identical positive charges (+1.0 C) on all four corners. Then, you put a negative charge (-2.0 C) exactly in the middle. We need to find the total pull on the center charge.

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1.  **Understand the Pull:** The center charge is negative (-). All corner charges are positive (+). Because opposites attract, the center charge is being pulled towards all four corners at the same time.
2.  **Look at the Distances:** Because the charge is exactly in the center of a perfect square, the distance to the top-left corner is exactly the same as the distance to the bottom-right corner.
3.  **The Tug-of-War:**
    *   The top-right corner pulls the center charge up and to the right.
    *   The bottom-left corner pulls the center charge down and to the left with the *exact same strength*.
    *   These two opposite pulls cancel each other out perfectly. It's a tie.
    *   The same thing happens with the top-left and bottom-right corners. They also cancel out.
4.  **The Result:** Since every pull is perfectly canceled by an identical pull in the exact opposite direction, the center charge doesn't move. The net force (total force) is zero.

**Final Answer:**
$$ \vec{F}_{\text{net}} = 0 \text{ N} $$
