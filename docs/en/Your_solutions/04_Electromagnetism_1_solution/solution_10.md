

## 10. Lorentz Force acting on Wire
**The Problem:** Instead of a single flying particle, we have a whole physical wire that is 2.0 meters long. Electricity (Current = 10 Amps) is flowing through it. It is sitting in a magnetic field of 0.5 Tesla. We need to find the force pushing on the wire at three different angles.

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
The formula for force on a wire is:
$$ F = I \cdot L \cdot B \cdot \sin(\theta) $$
*   $I$ = Current (10 A)
*   $L$ = Length of wire (2.0 m)
*   $B$ = Magnetic field (0.5 T)
*   $\theta$ (theta) = The angle.

Let's calculate $I \cdot L \cdot B$ first to make it easy:
$$ 10 \times 2.0 \times 0.5 = 10 $$
Now we just multiply 10 by the sine of each angle.

*   **a) $90^\circ$ (Perpendicular):**
    The wire is cutting directly across the magnetic field. This causes maximum force.
    $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$
    $$ F = 10 \times 1 = 10 \text{ N} $$

*   **b) $45^\circ$ (Diagonal):**
    The wire is sitting at a slant.
    $\sin(45^\circ) \approx 0.707$
    $$ F = 10 \times 0.707 = 7.07 \text{ N} $$

*   **c) $0^\circ$ (Parallel):**
    The wire is perfectly lined up with the magnetic field. Because it isn't "cutting" across the field lines, there is absolutely no magnetic force at all.
    $\sin(0^\circ) = 0$
    $$ F = 10 \times 0 = 0 \text{ N} $$
