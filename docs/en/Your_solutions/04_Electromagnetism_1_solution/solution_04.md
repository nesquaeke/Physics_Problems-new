

## 4. Force Comparison
**The Problem:** Inside an atom, an electron (-) and a proton (+) are separated by a tiny distance ($5.3 \times 10^{-11}$ meters). Because they have mass, gravity pulls them together. Because they have opposite charges, electric force pulls them together. We need to calculate both pulls and compare them.

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1.  **Calculate Electric Force ($F_e$):**
    We use Coulomb's Law. The charge of a proton and electron is a known tiny number: $e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}$.
    $$ F_e = k \frac{q_1 \cdot q_2}{r^2} $$
    $$ F_e = 9 \times 10^9 \frac{(1.6 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot (1.6 \times 10^{-19})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2} \approx 8.2 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N} $$
2.  **Calculate Gravitational Force ($F_g$):**
    We use Newton's Law of Gravity. We need standard constants: Gravity ($G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}$), electron mass ($m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}$ kg), proton mass ($m_p = 1.67 \times 10^{-27}$ kg).
    $$ F_g = G \frac{m_e \cdot m_p}{r^2} $$
    $$ F_g = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31}) \cdot (1.67 \times 10^{-27})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2} \approx 3.6 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N} $$
3.  **Compare them (Find the Ratio):**
    To see how much bigger the electric force is, we divide $F_e$ by $F_g$.
    $$ \text{Ratio} = \frac{F_e}{F_g} = \frac{8.2 \times 10^{-8}}{3.6 \times 10^{-47}} \approx 2.3 \times 10^{39} $$

**Final Answer:** The ratio $F_e/F_g$ is $2.3 \times 10^{39}$. This means the electric force is so incredibly strong that gravity is practically invisible on an atomic level!
