
## 8. Lorentz Force
**The Problem:** A tiny particle with a specific charge and mass is flying at 1 million m/s. It flies directly across (perpendicular to) a magnetic field. We just need to find how hard the magnetic field pushes the particle. This push is called the "Lorentz Force".

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1.  **The Formula:** The force on a moving charge in a magnetic field is:
    $$ F = q \cdot v \cdot B \cdot \sin(\theta) $$
    *   $q$ = charge ($2 \times 10^{-19}$ C)
    *   $v$ = speed ($10^6$ m/s)
    *   $B$ = magnetic field strength (0.5 T)
    *   $\theta$ (theta) = the angle the particle hits the field.
2.  **The Angle:** The problem says "perpendicular". In math, perpendicular means exactly 90 degrees. The sine of 90 degrees is exactly 1. ($\sin(90^\circ) = 1$). This means the force is at its absolute maximum.
3.  **Multiply it all together:**
    $$ F = (2 \times 10^{-19}) \times (10^6) \times (0.5) \times 1 $$
    $$ F = 1 \times 10^{-13} \text{ Newtons} $$

**Final Answer:** The Lorentz force acting on the particle is $1 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}$.
