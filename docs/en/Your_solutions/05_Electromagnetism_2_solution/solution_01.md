# Section 5: Electromagnetism II - Detailed Solutions

**Simple Dictionary for This Section:**
*   **Flux ($\Phi$):** Imagine a lightbulb inside a paper box. Flux is simply "how much light" is punching through the paper. In physics, it's how much "electric wind" passes through a surface.
*   **Current ($I$):** The flow of electricity in a wire. Like water flowing in a pipe. Measured in Amperes (A).
*   **Magnetic Field ($B$):** The invisible magnetic aura created by magnets or wires. Measured in Teslas (T).
*   **Wavelength ($\lambda$):** The physical distance between the tops of two waves in the ocean.
*   **Frequency ($f$):** How many waves hit the beach in one second.

**Important Constants:**
*   Vacuum permittivity ($\epsilon_0$): $8.85 \times 10^{-12} \text{ F/m}$
*   Vacuum permeability ($\mu_0$): $4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$
*   Speed of light ($c$): $3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

---

## 1. Gauss's Law
**The Problem:** We put a $+2 \text{ C}$ charge (like a bright glowing ball) exactly in the center of an invisible sphere that has a 1-meter radius. We need to find the "Electric Flux" passing through this sphere.

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1.  **The Concept:** Gauss's Law is actually a brilliant shortcut. It says: "I don't care how big the sphere is, or what shape the box is. The total amount of electric wind (Flux) coming out depends ONLY on the amount of charge trapped inside."
2.  **The Formula:** 
    Flux ($\Phi$) = Charge enclosed ($Q$) divided by a constant number of nature ($\epsilon_0$).
    
    $$ \Phi = \frac{Q_{\text{enclosed}}}{\epsilon_0} $$

3.  **The Calculation:**
    Notice that the "1-meter radius" information is a trap! We don't even need to use it. We just take the charge (2) and divide it by the constant.

    $$ \Phi = \frac{2}{8.85 \times 10^{-12}} \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C} $$

**Final Answer:** The total electric flux is $2.26 \times 10^{11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}$.
