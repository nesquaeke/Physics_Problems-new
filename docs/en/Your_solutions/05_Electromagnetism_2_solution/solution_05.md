
## 5. Energy Stored by charge in a capacitor
**The Problem:** Imagine two metal plates sitting very close to each other without touching. This is a "Capacitor", a device used to store electrical energy (like a fast battery).
*   Area of plates ($S$) = $0.02 \text{ m}^2$
*   Distance between them ($d$) = $5 \text{ mm} = 0.005 \text{ m}$
*   Voltage applied ($V$) = $500 \text{ V}$ *(Note: I use 'V' for Voltage so we don't confuse it with 'U' for Energy).*

**Step-by-step Explanation:**

**Part 1: Capacitance ($C$)**
*   **Concept:** Capacitance is simply "how big the storage tank is". Bigger plates and closer distance mean a bigger tank.
*   **Formula:** $C = \epsilon_0 \frac{S}{d}$
    
    $$ C = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \frac{0.02}{0.005} = 35.4 \times 10^{-12} \text{ Farads} $$

**Part 2: Energy Stored ($U$)**
*   **Concept:** How much electrical "juice" is actually stored in the tank right now.
*   **Formula:** $U = \frac{1}{2} C V^2$
    
    $$ U = 0.5 \times (35.4 \times 10^{-12}) \times (500)^2 = 4.425 \times 10^{-6} \text{ Joules} $$

**Part 3: Electric Field ($E$)**
*   **Concept:** The intensity of the invisible electric "wind" blowing straight from one plate to the other across the tiny gap.
*   **Formula:** $E = \frac{\text{Voltage}}{\text{Distance}} = \frac{V}{d}$
    
    $$ E = \frac{500}{0.005} = 100,000 \text{ V/m} $$

**Part 4: Force of attraction ($F$)**
*   **Concept:** Because one plate is filled with positive charge and the other with negative charge, they try to pull each other together like magnets.
*   **Formula:** $F = \frac{1}{2} \epsilon_0 E^2 S$
    
    $$ F = 0.5 \times (8.85 \times 10^{-12}) \times (100,000)^2 \times 0.02 \approx 8.85 \times 10^{-4} \text{ Newtons} $$
