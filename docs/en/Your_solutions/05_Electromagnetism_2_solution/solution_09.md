## 9. Refraction (Snell's Law)
**The Problem:** A laser beam is shooting through the air and hits a thick piece of glass at a $30^\circ$ angle. Because glass is thicker than air, the light will slow down and "bend". What is the new angle of the light inside the glass?

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1.  **The Concept:** Imagine pushing a shopping cart from smooth concrete into thick mud at an angle. The wheel that hits the mud first slows down, causing the whole cart to turn sharply. That's what light does.
2.  **The Formula (Snell's Law):**
    
    $$ n_1 \cdot \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \cdot \sin(\theta_2) $$
    
    *   $n_1$ = thickness of air ($1.00$)
    *   $\theta_1$ = incoming angle ($30^\circ$)
    *   $n_2$ = thickness of glass ($1.50$)
    *   $\theta_2$ = the bent angle we want to find.
3.  **The Calculation:**
    
    $$ 1.00 \times \sin(30^\circ) = 1.50 \times \sin(\theta_2) $$
    
    We know $\sin(30^\circ)$ is $0.5$.
    
    $$ 0.5 = 1.50 \times \sin(\theta_2) $$
    
    Divide by $1.50$:
    
    $$ \sin(\theta_2) = \frac{0.5}{1.50} = \frac{1}{3} $$
    
    To find the actual angle from the sine, we use the inverse sine function (arcsin) on a calculator.
    
    $$ \theta_2 = \arcsin(1/3) \approx 19.47^\circ $$

**Final Answer:** The light bends to an angle of $19.47^\circ$ inside the glass.
