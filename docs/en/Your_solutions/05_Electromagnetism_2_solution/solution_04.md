

## 4. Magnetic Torque
**The Problem:** We have a wire bent into a rectangle ($10 \text{ cm}$ by $5 \text{ cm}$). Electricity ($2 \text{ A}$) flows in loops around this rectangle. We place this loop inside a big magnetic field ($B = 0.3 \text{ T}$). The field lines are running parallel to the table the loop is sitting on. We want to find the "Torque" (the twisting force that wants to make the loop flip over).

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1.  **The Concept:** A loop of wire with electricity running through it acts exactly like a bar magnet. If you put a magnet inside another magnetic field, it will try to spin and line up perfectly. This spinning force is Torque ($\tau$).
2.  **Find the Area ($A$):** 
    First, convert cm to meters ($0.1 \text{ m}$ by $0.05 \text{ m}$).
    
    $$ \text{Area} = 0.1 \times 0.05 = 0.005 \text{ m}^2 $$

3.  **The Angle Trick ($\theta$):**
    In physics, the angle for a loop is NOT the angle of the wire itself. It is the angle of a "stick" pointing straight out of the center of the loop (the normal vector). If the magnetic field is running flat across the table (parallel to the loop), then that "stick" is standing straight up, making a perfect $90^\circ$ angle with the magnetic field.
    So, $\theta = 90^\circ$, and $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.
4.  **The Formula and Calculation:**
    
    $$ \text{Torque} (\tau) = I \cdot A \cdot B \cdot \sin(\theta) $$
    
    $$ \tau = 2 \times 0.005 \times 0.3 \times 1 = 0.003 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m} $$

**Final Answer:** The magnetic torque on the loop is $0.003 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}$.
