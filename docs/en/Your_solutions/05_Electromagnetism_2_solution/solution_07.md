

## 7. Wavelength and Frequency
**The Problem:** The human eye loves light that has a wavelength of $550 \text{ nm}$ ($550 \times 10^{-9}$ meters). What color is this, and what is its frequency?

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1.  **The Color:** This is purely a memorization fact in physics. The visible light spectrum goes from 400 nm (Purple) to 700 nm (Red). Exactly in the middle, around 550 nm, is the color **Green**.
2.  **The Frequency ($f$):** Frequency means "how many waves hit my eye every single second". The formula connects speed, wavelength, and frequency: $c = \lambda \cdot f$. We rearrange it to find $f$:
    
    $$ f = \frac{c}{\lambda} $$
    
    $$ f = \frac{3 \times 10^8}{550 \times 10^{-9}} \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz} $$
    *(This means 545 trillion waves enter your eye every second when you look at a green leaf!)*
