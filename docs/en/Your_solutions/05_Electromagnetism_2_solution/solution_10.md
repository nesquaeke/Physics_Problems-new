

## 10. Speed of Light in Media
**The Problem:** Light travels at its maximum speed ($3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$) in empty space. A diamond is extremely dense and hard. Its "thickness index" ($n$) is $2.42$. How fast does light travel when it gets trapped inside a diamond?

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1.  **The Formula:** To find the speed of light in any material ($v$), just take the absolute maximum speed ($c$) and divide it by the material's thickness index ($n$).
    
    $$ v = \frac{c}{n} $$

2.  **The Calculation:**
    
    $$ v = \frac{3 \times 10^8}{2.42} \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s} $$

**Final Answer:** Light slows down drastically inside a diamond, travelling at roughly $1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$ (which is less than half its normal speed!).
