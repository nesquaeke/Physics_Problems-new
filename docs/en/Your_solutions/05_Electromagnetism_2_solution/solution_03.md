

## 3. Biot-Savart Law
**The Problem:** Instead of a long wire, we only have a tiny piece of a wire (a "segment") that is $0.1 \text{ m}$ long. It carries $3 \text{ A}$ of current. We want to find the magnetic field it creates at a point $P$, which is $0.2 \text{ m}$ away. The point $P$ is exactly perpendicular to the wire.

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1.  **The Concept:** The Biot-Savart Law is just like Ampere's law, but it is used for tiny, broken pieces of wire instead of infinitely long wires.
2.  **The Formula:**
    
    $$ B = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I \cdot L \cdot \sin(\theta)}{r^2} $$
    
    *   $I$ = Current ($3 \text{ A}$)
    *   $L$ = Length of the piece ($0.1 \text{ m}$)
    *   $r$ = Distance to point ($0.2 \text{ m}$)
    *   $\theta$ = Angle. The problem says "perpendicular", which means exactly $90^\circ$. And $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.
3.  **The Calculation:**
    
    $$ B = (10^{-7}) \frac{3 \times 0.1 \times 1}{(0.2)^2} $$
    
    $$ B = 10^{-7} \frac{0.3}{0.04} = 7.5 \times 10^{-7} \text{ T} $$

**Final Answer:** The magnetic field at point P is $7.5 \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}$.
