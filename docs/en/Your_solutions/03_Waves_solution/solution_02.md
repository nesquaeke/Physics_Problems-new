2. String Harmonics
Concept: When you pluck a guitar string, it vibrates. The "fundamental frequency" means the string is making its simplest vibration—just one big jump up and down in the middle. This shape is exactly half of a full wave.Step-by-Step Solution:Since the string length ($L$) holds half a wave, a full wavelength ($\lambda$) is twice the length of the string:$$\lambda = 2 \cdot L$$Convert the length to meters: $64$ cm $= 0.64$ m.$$\lambda = 2 \cdot 0.64 = 1.28 \text{ meters}$$Now, use the wave speed formula ($v = \lambda \cdot f$) with the given frequency ($330$ Hz):$$v = 1.28 \cdot 330 = 422.4 \text{ m/s}$$The wave travels at $422.4$ m/s on the string.
