6. Wave EquationConcept:
 A wave equation looks scary, but it's just a template. The standard template is:$$y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)$$Compare our equation $y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)$ to the template to easily steal the numbers!Step-by-Step Solution:a) Amplitude ($A$): The number in front.$A = 0.05$ mb) Wavelength ($\lambda$): The number next to $x$ is $k$. Here, $k = 2\pi$. Since $k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$, that means:$$  2\pi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \implies \lambda = 1 \text{ m}$$c) Frequency ($f$): The number next to $t$ is $\omega$. Here, $\omega = 50\pi$. Since $\omega = 2\pi f$, we do:$$  50\pi = 2\pi f \implies f = 25 \text{ Hz}$$d) Wave speed ($v$): Just multiply wavelength and frequency:$$  v = \lambda \cdot f = 1 \cdot 25 = 25 \text{ m/s}$$
