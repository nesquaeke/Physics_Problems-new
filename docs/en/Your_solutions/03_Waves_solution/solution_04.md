4. Phase Difference
Concept: A full wave is a full circle, which is $360^\circ$ or $2\pi$ radians. If two points are separated by a certain distance, they are at different parts of the wave's cycle.Step-by-Step Solution:The formula for phase difference ($\Delta \phi$) is the wave number ($k$) times the distance ($\Delta x$):$$\Delta \phi = k \cdot \Delta x$$Remember that $k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$ and our distance is $\Delta x = \frac{\lambda}{3}$.Multiply them together:$$\Delta \phi = \left(\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{\lambda}{3}\right) = \frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ radians}$$
