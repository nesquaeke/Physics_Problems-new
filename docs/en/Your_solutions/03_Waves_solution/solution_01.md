Wave PropertiesConcept: Imagine a wave as a snake wiggling.Frequency ($f$): How many times the snake wiggles in one second (measured in Hertz, Hz).Speed ($v$): How fast the wave travels forward (measured in m/s).Wavelength ($\lambda$): The length of one complete wiggle (measured in meters).The magic formula connecting them is:$$v = \lambda \cdot f$$To find the wavelength, we just rearrange it:$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$Step-by-Step Solution:In Air: We know the speed in air is $343$ m/s and the frequency is $440$ Hz.$$\lambda_{air} = \frac{343}{440} \approx 0.78 \text{ meters}$$In Water: The speed changes in water ($1482$ m/s), but the frequency stays the same because the source making the sound doesn't change.$$\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482}{440} \approx 3.37 \text{ meters}$$
