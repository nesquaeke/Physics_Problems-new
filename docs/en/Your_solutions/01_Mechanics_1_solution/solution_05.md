## 5) Relative Velocity

Given:
- river flows east at `2 m/s`
- boat speed in still water is `5 m/s`
- boat wants to go directly north
- river width is `200 m`

We want:
- direction the boat should head
- crossing time

---

### Step 1: Cancel river flow

The river pushes the boat east at `2 m/s`.

To go directly north, the boat must aim a little west of north, so its westward component cancels the river flow.

Let `theta` be the angle west of north.

Then:

`5 sin(theta) = 2`

So:

`sin(theta) = 2/5 = 0.4`

`theta = sin^-1(0.4) ≈ 23.6 degrees`

**Direction:** `23.6 degrees west of north`

---

### Step 2: Find northward speed

The northward component is:

`5 cos(theta)`

`5 cos(23.6 deg) ≈ 5 * 0.9165 = 4.58 m/s`

So the effective crossing speed is:

`4.58 m/s`

---

### Step 3: Crossing time

`time = distance / speed`

`time = 200 / 4.58`

`time ≈ 43.7 s`

**Final answers:**
- Direction: `23.6 degrees west of north`
- Crossing time: `43.7 s`
