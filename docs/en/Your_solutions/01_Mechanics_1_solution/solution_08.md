## 8) Circular Motion

We want the centripetal acceleration of a person standing on the Earth's equator.

Given:
- Earth's radius: `R = 6378 km = 6.378 x 10^6 m`
- Earth's rotation period: `T = 24 h = 86400 s`

Centripetal acceleration is:

`a_c = v^2 / R`

But first find speed:

`v = 2*pi*R / T`

So:

`v = 2*pi*(6.378 x 10^6) / 86400`

`v ≈ 463.8 m/s`

Now:

`a_c = v^2 / R`

`a_c = (463.8)^2 / (6.378 x 10^6)`

`a_c ≈ 0.0337 m/s^2`

**Final answer:** `0.0337 m/s^2`

### Interpretation

This is much smaller than normal gravity (`9.81 m/s^2`), which is why we do not strongly feel Earth's rotation.
